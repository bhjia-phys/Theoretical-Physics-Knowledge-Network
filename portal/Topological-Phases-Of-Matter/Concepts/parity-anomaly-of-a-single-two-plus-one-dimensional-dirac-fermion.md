## Summary
A single 2+1-dimensional Dirac fermion cannot be regulated so as to preserve both large-gauge invariance and parity; choosing gauge invariance leaves a parity-odd Chern-Simons term whose coefficient is half-integer modulo integers.

## Scope
Background concept needed to interpret the half-level Chern-Simons response of a massive Dirac cone.

## Regime
Continuum odd-dimensional fermion effective action with large-gauge transformations included.

## Assumptions
- `A single charged Dirac fermion in 2+1 dimensions is coupled to a background U(1) gauge field.`
- `The low-energy theory is considered modulo integer counterterms in the Chern-Simons level.`
