## Summary
For abelian U(1) Chern-Simons theory, a gauge transformation A -> A + dLambda changes the action by a boundary integral, which is the anomaly-inflow term on manifolds with boundary.

## Scope
Minimal calculation behind the anomaly-inflow argument for chiral edge modes.

## Regime
Abelian 2+1-dimensional Chern-Simons response on a manifold that may have boundary.

## Assumptions
- `The bulk gauge field is abelian.`
- `Boundary contributions are retained instead of discarded.`
