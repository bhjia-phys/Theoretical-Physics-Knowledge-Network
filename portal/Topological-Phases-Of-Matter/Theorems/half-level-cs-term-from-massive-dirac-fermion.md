## Summary
Integrating out a single massive 2+1-dimensional Dirac fermion produces the parity-odd response (sign m / 2) I_CS(A); the half-integer coefficient is fixed by the hemisphere Berry-flux calculation of the occupied Dirac band.

## Scope
Reusable theorem-level statement of the half-level response that feeds the Lecture Three edge-mode story and the Haldane bridge.

## Regime
Continuum parity-anomaly analysis of one massive Dirac cone.

- `The statement is continuum and low-energy; microscopic lattice completion is treated separately.`

## Mathematical Content
- `kind=display` | `label=utof` | Massive charged Dirac fermion coupled to the background gauge field.
$$
I_{\psi}=\int \mathrm d^3x\,\bar\psi(\slashed D-m)\psi
$$
- `kind=display` | `label=uftof` | Parity-odd low-energy response generated by integrating out the gapped fermion.
$$
\Gamma_{\mathrm{odd}}[A]=\frac{\operatorname{sign}m}{2}\,I_{\mathrm{CS}}(A)=\frac{\operatorname{sign}m}{2}\,\frac{1}{4\pi}\int \epsilon^{ijk}A_i\partial_jA_k\,\mathrm d^3x
$$
- `kind=display` | `label=puft` | Single-particle Hamiltonian whose occupied-band topology fixes the sign and one-half factor.
$$
H=\sigma_x p_x+\sigma_y p_y+m\sigma_z
$$
